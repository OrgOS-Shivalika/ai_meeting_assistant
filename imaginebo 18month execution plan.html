00 The two numbers that govern everything

50K sellers is the lever. 2.5M SMBs is the yield.

MilestoneTargetWhen

Read this first: 50K sellers is the M18 lever. 2.5M SMBs is not an M18 outcome — it is the downstream yield of a mature selling base. We build the machine (DSAs + 50K sellers) by M18; the machine produces 2.5M over the following 12–18 months.

The structural shift — solving both constraints

Today: ₹4,000 paid → 5 engaged partners = ₹800 CAC/partner, paid upfront.

DSA model: you sign a DSA firm, and its existing salaried sellers become your engaged sellers on day one — no per-seller acquisition cost, no training-from-zero. You pay the DSA commission on production; the DSA pays and manages its own employees. Upfront seller-CAC → near zero, and the DSA's management layer absorbs supervision that would otherwise need imagine.bo headcount.

Highest-leverage move

The DSAs you're already building the HDFC lending agent for are the same firms — established sales teams with warm SMB books. Same agent, same customer, second product (website + loan). Recruit that network first.

03 Phase execution

Three phases — prove, scale, compound.

Phase I · M1–M6

Foundation & Proof

40 → 1,800 sellers

Objective: build the agent stack, sign the first ~60 DSAs (lead with the HDFC lending-DSA network), prove one DSA-onboarding playbook is repeatable. Do not scale spend until the playbook converts.

Node build sequence
  • M1–2Stand up Setu (sourcing lending/insurance DSA firms) + Diksha (cohort onboarding — a DSA's whole team in one batch). First 2–5 anchor DSAs signed by founders, prioritising the HDFC DSA orbit.
  • M2–3Sampark FSM live (template-show → capture). Nirmaan wired to one-iteration loop. Sauda v1 paywall with payment links — kill the pre-value paywall.
  • M4–5Khoj lead-distribution live (30-day give-then-earn enforced). Chakra referral loop instrumented.
  • M6Tainat + Vriddhi v1. Sutradhaar runs the full chain end-to-end for one DSA cohort, no manual hand-offs.
DSA motion — the engine

Founders + first BD hire close DSA firms face-to-face. Offer = production payout (§04) + co-branded enablement + a ready agent stack their sellers plug into. Sign agreements, not handshakes. Each anchor commits its team + a target (a 15-seller DSA → 30+ SMBs in 90 days). The DSA's managers run their own people.

04 Unit economics & commission

One payout to the DSA. No master layer.

You pay one entity — the DSA firm — which compensates its own employees. The previously-separate "seller 10%" and "recruiter 30% override" collapse into a single DSA payout, because the DSA is simultaneously the employer of sellers and the contracting partner.

The override sunset is your margin engine

The 30% acquisition slice applies to each SMB's first 12 months — rolling, not a calendar cutoff. It's expensive exactly when you want selling velocity (Phases I–II, when nearly every SMB is fresh Year-1) and auto-decays into margin in Phase III as early cohorts cross 12 months. Forecast it as a 12-month trailing liability per SMB cohort — peaks in Phase II, inflects down in Phase III.

Margin sensitivity

Year-1 contribution is ~₹218/SMB. Through M18 the base is mostly Year-1 (S-curve → most SMBs closed in M13–18 are brand new), so blended contribution ≈ ~₹215/SMB through M18, expanding toward ₹362 as cohorts age past 12 months (~M24+). The model is volume- and churn-sensitive until the base ages. Two levers widen margin earlier: Vriddhi upsell lifting ARPU above ₹480, and the override sunset.

One number to set — payout depth

40% Y1 / 10% trail (recommended): matches your locked economics; leaves the DSA enough to pay salaried sellers + margin — essential, since DSA sellers are employees, not commission-only. → imagine.bo ₹218 Y1 (45%).

30% Y1 / 10% trail (leaner): lifts imagine.bo to ₹266 Y1 (55%), but may make it hard for a DSA to cover salaries at low volume → slower seller activation. Use only for high-volume DSAs.

08 Thesis

Build the DSA recruitment machine, not the customer base, in 18 months. DSAs — firms with their own salaried sellers, led by the lending-DSA network you're already building the HDFC agent for — convert a capital-fatal ₹40M upfront-CAC problem into a single production-based payout that scales behind revenue, while the DSAs' own managers supervise their people so your team never balloons. Ten agent-default nodes under Sutradhaar let just 26 people operate 50K sellers; the only humans who matter at scale are the ~4 signing DSAs. Fix the paywall ordering and instrument the referral loop early — by Phase III those two drive >40% of growth. The 40% Year-1 payout is front-loaded for velocity and auto-decays into margin as each cohort crosses 12 months — Year-1 contribution is deliberately thin (~₹218/SMB) and the model is volume- and churn-sensitive until cohorts age. 50K sellers by M18; 2.5M SMBs as that base matures through M30–36.
